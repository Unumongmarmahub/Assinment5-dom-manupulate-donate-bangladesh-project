Donate for Flood at Noakhali, Bangladesh

In August 2024, Noakhali district in Bangladesh experienced severe flooding due to continuous heavy rainfall since mid-July. Many residents were forced to take shelter on rooftops as floodwaters reached waist level in some areas. Relief efforts were hampered by damaged infrastructure, making it difficult to deliver essential supplies2. The floods caused significant damage, with an estimated loss of Tk533.59 crore and 18 reported deaths2.

Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

In 2024, Bangladesh witnessed a significant Quota Reform Movement driven by students and youth activists demanding a fair and merit-based system in public sector recruitment. The movement gained momentum after the reinstatement of the pre-2018 quota system, which was declared illegal by the High Court2. Protests, public demonstrations, and traffic obstructions became common as students voiced their dissent. The movement faced severe backlash, including police blockades, internet shutdowns, and curfews2. Despite the challenges, the movement succeeded in pressuring the government to reform the quota system, ensuring 93% of government job recruitments would be based on merit.
